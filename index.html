Join us to celebrate together in Manila, Philippines.

Whether it’s your first time or a return visit, this vibrant country offers endless warmth, history, and hospitality.

Before You Go:

  • Currency: Philippine Peso (₱); credit cards widely accepted.
  • Transport: Grab (local Uber) is the easiest and safest way to get around.
  • Power plugs: Type A/B (same as Canada/USA); 220V voltage.

Wedding Day Travel Tips

The wedding venue is in Rizal, about 35 km from Manila. Traffic in Manila can be unpredictable and heavy, especially during rush hour, so expect the drive to take at least 1.5 hours. Plan to arrive early to avoid delays and travel stress.

FAQ

Dress Code

  • Formal or semi-formal attire (no casual wear)
  • Color Theme: Shades of Blue — navy, sky, dusty, or pastel
  • Men: Suit or Barong Tagalog
  • Women: Gown, midi or cocktail dress, or elegant jumpsuit (not white)

Weather in January

  • Sunny days with mild temperatures around 24–30°C (75–86°F).
  • Wear light, breezy clothes, sunglasses, and sunscreen (a fan or parasol earns you bonus points).
  • Pack a light layer for the occasional cool evening.

Where to Stay

  • Bonifacio Global City (BGC): Modern, walkable, full of restaurants and hotels like Shangri-La The Fort or Grand Hyatt.
  • Makati: Great shopping and nightlife, with hotels like Fairmont, New World, and The Peninsula.
  • Pasay: Close to the airport and Manila Bay, with hotels such as Conrad and Sofitel.

Things to Do in Manila

  • Intramuros: Historic walled city with cobblestone streets.
  • National Museum Complex: Showcases Philippine art and history.
  • Mall of Asia: Asia’s largest shopping and entertainment hub.
  • Bonifacio Global City (BGC): Modern district with cafes and nightlife.

Explore Beyond Manila

  • Tagaytay: Cool weather, scenic views, and cozy cafés (2 hours away).
  • Batangas: Beaches and diving spots (2–3 hours).
  • Palawan: Lagoons and island-hopping adventures (1-hour flight).
  • Boracay: World-famous white sand and vibrant nightlife.
  • Siargao: Surfer’s paradise with stunning coastal scenery.

What to Eat in Manila

Must-Try Filipino Dishes

  • Adobo – soy-vinegar braised chicken or pork.
  • Sinigang – sour tamarind soup with pork, shrimp, or fish.
  • Lechon – crispy roasted pig, a Filipino celebration favorite.
  • Kare-Kare – peanut stew with oxtail and vegetables.
  • Sisig – sizzling crispy pork with calamansi and egg.

Must-Try Desserts & Drinks

  • Halo-Halo – shaved ice with fruits, jellies, and ube ice cream.
  • Mango Float – layered graham dessert with sweet mangoes.
  • Leche Flan – rich caramel custard.
  • Buko Shake – refreshing young coconut smoothie.
  • Ube Desserts – sweet purple yam in cakes or pastries.

Fast-Food Must-Try

  • Jollibee – iconic Filipino fast food!
  • Mang Inasal – grilled chicken and unlimited rice.
  • Chowking – Filipino-Chinese rice meals and dim sum.

Breakfast & Local Coffee

  • Barako Coffee – bold, strong, and proudly Filipino.
  • Tapsilog – beef + egg + garlic rice.
  • Longsilog – sweet sausage + egg + garlic rice.
  • Bangsilog – milkfish + egg + garlic rice.
