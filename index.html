To help you prepare for the wedding and make the most of your time in the Philippines, we’ve included some essential tips below. For more details, check out the FAQ section further down the page.

Essentials To Know Before You Go:

  • Currency: Philippine Peso (₱); credit cards are widely accepted
  • Power Plugs: Type A/B (same as Canada/USA); voltage is 220V
  • Transport: Grab (local ride-hailing app) is the easiest and safest way to get around
  • Phone & Internet: Get a local SIM (Smart or Globe) at Manila Airport or in the city (passport required), or an eSIM via Airalo — use our Airalo referral code EVGENI8160 for a discount

Wedding Day Travel Tips

The wedding venue is in Rizal, about 35 km from Manila centre. Traffic in Manila can be unpredictable and heavy, especially during rush hour, so expect the drive to take at least 1.5 hours. Plan to arrive early to avoid delays and travel stress.

Doors open at 3 PM & close at 3:30 PM.

FAQ

Dress Code

  • Attire: Formal or semi-formal (no casual wear)
  • Palette: All shades of blue or green. Colour matching is optional; avoid white
  • Women: Gown, midi or cocktail dress, or elegant jumpsuit
  • Men: Suit or Barong Tagalog

Weather in January

  • Sunny days with mild temperatures around 24–30°C (75–86°F)
  • Bring summer wear, sunglasses, and sunscreen (bonus points for a fan or parasol)
  • Pack a light layer for the occasional cool evening

Where to Stay

  • Bonifacio Global City (BGC): Modern, walkable hub with it all — consider Seda or Red Planet
  • Makati: The business and shopping hub, known for great nightlife and hotels
  • Pasay: Conveniently located near the airport and Manila Bay

Things to Do in Manila

  • Intramuros: Historic walled city with cobblestone streets
  • Rizal Park: Iconic landmark beside Intramuros
  • Binondo: World’s oldest Chinatown, known for food and culture
  • National Museum Complex: Showcases Philippine art and history
  • Mall of Asia: Local go-to for dining, entertainment, and cooling off
  • Bonifacio Global City (BGC): Trendy district for dining, shopping, and nightlife

Explore Beyond Manila

Tropical Islands 1-hour flight away

  • Palawan: Stunning lagoons and island-hopping in El Nido and Coron
  • Boracay: World-famous white-sand beaches and lively nightlife

Road Trips from Manila

  • Tagaytay: Cool climate with scenic views of Taal Volcano and Lake
  • Batangas: Mountain scenery and Laiya Beach (2–3.5-hour drive)

What to Eat in the Philippines

Filipino Favorites

  • Adobo: Soy-vinegar braised chicken or pork
  • Sinigang: Tangy tamarind soup
  • Lumpia: Filipino spring rolls
  • Lechon: Crispy roasted pork
  • Kare-Kare: Peanut stew with oxtail
  • Inihaw: Grilled meats & seafood
  • Pancit: Stir-fried noodles, a celebration staple

Must-Try Desserts

  • Halo-Halo: Shaved ice with fruits & ube ice cream
  • Buko Shake: Fresh coconut smoothie
  • Ube Treats: Purple yam cakes & pastries
  • Leche Flan: Creamy caramel custard
  • Puto: Steamed rice cake
  • Biko: Sweet sticky rice
  • Turon: Fried banana spring roll
  • Pandesal: Soft bread roll, sweet or savory
  • Carabao Mango: Often called the world’s best

Local Fast Food

  • Jollibee: Iconic Filipino fast food
  • Mang Inasal: Grilled chicken & unlimited rice
  • Chowking: Filipino-Chinese comfort meals

Traditional Breakfast

  • Tapsilog: Beef, egg & garlic rice
  • Longsilog: Sweet sausage, egg & garlic rice
  • Bangsilog: Milkfish, egg & garlic rice
  • Barako Coffee: Bold, proudly local brew
  • Champorado: Chocolate rice porridge
